Oslob, Cebu



Whenever people ask me what my favorite travel destination is, I always tell them it's Oslob, Cebu. And when I show them the underwater picture of me next to a whaleshark, without fail, people love it! Oslob is not only known for swimming with gentle giants, but it also has beautiful white sand bars and coral reefs. Plus, in some places the water is so cler that you can see the bottom of the seabed.

What to do in Oslob, Cebu: Swimming with Whalesharks, Island Hopping, Snorkeling


Alaminos, Pangasinan



Home of the famous Hundred Islands, Alaminos is the Philippine tourist spot classic. You need to wake up very early, about 6, to prepare for Island Hopping at 7. Boats take you across many islands on a full-day tour, and they will even prepare a bountiful seafood lunch for your family on one of the islands.

What to do in Oslob, Cebu: Island Hopping, Snorkeling, Seafood lunch


Padres Point, Batanes



Batanes is the very tip of the Philippine islands in the north. It's a peaceful place with a very low crime rate. And because the region is small, they say all the locals know each other. The view is stunning, and at Padres Point, you can look out onto the sea that eventually stretches towards Taiwan.

What to do in Padres Point, Batanes: Lookout, Photo-opt, Sunbathing.
